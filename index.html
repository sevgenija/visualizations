Your class of potential risks from chemotherapy is 3.

You are allocated to the risk group 3 in regard to potential side effects from chemotherapy. This means that in general people from the same risk group as yours experience several problems affecting their lifestyles.

Chemotherapy kills fast-growing cancer cells. But when the drugs travel throughout the body they affect healthy cells as well. The side effects related to chemotherapy are caused by the damage of these normal healthy cells. The side effects can vary, depending on the drug or combination of them. However, the most common ones, people in the same risk group as you might experience, are listed here.

  • Insomnia. Sleep problems such as being unable to fall asleep and/or stay asleep, also called insomnia, are common in your risk. Long-term insomnia might also cause depression and icrease anxiety.
  • Peripheral neuropathy. Peripheral neuropathy is a result of damage to the peripheral nerves. These nerves carry information from the brain to other parts of the body. Damage of these nerves can cause tingling, numbness, or a pins-and-needles feeling in your feet and hands, inability to feel pain or loosing hot and cold perception. Symptoms usually disappear after the treatment but there is a risk that neuropathy will have a long-term effect and turn into chronic peripheral neuropathy.

Some side effects disappear quite quickly, but for some of them it can take months or years to disappear completely. While side effects can be unpleasant, they must be accessed against the need to kill the cancer cells.
